It started
with a craving

Ma'am Saab began as a craving, for the food of back home, cooked the way it was meant to be: bold, generous, full of feeling. What started as a string of Charleston pop-ups in 2019 became a love letter to the subcontinent: the chaats of Karachi's markets, the karahis of the super highway, the slow nihari of old Lucknow, all replated with a Lowcountry drawl.

We cook modern Pakistani food with bold spice and a little swagger, and we host like family, generous, a little teasing, always proud of where we come from. From a borrowed kitchen to a permanent home on Meeting Street, the mission hasn't changed: make you feel something, and send you home a little more in love. Your table awaits...

A few words from our kitchen

From me to you

There is a chapli kabab on our menu that is my mother's. A Karachi biryani with a quiet secret tucked into the rice. A nihari that took me half a lifetime to understand, and now I cannot live without. None of it is here by accident.

Ma'am Saab is the table I always wished I could set, the one where the food tastes like home and nobody is in a hurry to leave. Everything on it is something I love. I am just glad you came to share it.

So who is Ma'am Saab? Every mother, daughter, sister, teacher and mentor who never took no for an answer and made it happen anyway. We all have a Ma'am Saab in our lives. We all have one in us. Yes, even the men.

Raheel Gauba, founder
